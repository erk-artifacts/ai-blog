## Anthropic's New "Claude Cowork" Feature Sends Shockwaves Through SaaS Industry

A new feature announced by AI development company Anthropic is creating significant ripples throughout the SaaS (Software-as-a-Service) industry. The new plugins for "Claude Cowork" now enable AI to directly operate external SaaS services.

This development has sparked concern that the various tools and services previously adopted individually by enterprises could be replaced by AI, causing major SaaS companies' stock prices to plummet. The market has even begun using the phrase "**the death of SaaS**."

However, Japanese SaaS development vendors are pushing back with statements like "there is SaaS that won't die," signaling that the industry is entering a major transition period.

**Impact on our lives:** The way enterprises use business software could change dramatically, with AI automating an increasing number of business processes.

## Nvidia CEO Scales Back Investment in OpenAI and Anthropic

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang announced that the company's investments in OpenAI and Anthropic will "likely be the last." However, his explanation leaves many questions unanswered.

While withdrawing from direct investment in AI companies, Nvidia will continue to engage with both companies through its most critical business area: GPU chips for data centers. How will this strategic shift impact capital flow in the AI industry?

[→ Read the original article (TechCrunch)](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/04/jensen-huang-says-nvidia-is-pulling-back-from-openai-and-anthropic-but-his-explanation-raises-more-questions-than-it-answers/)

**Impact on our lives:** Changes in the funding environment for AI companies could affect the pace and direction of new AI technology development.

## Anthropic's Dario Amodei Criticizes OpenAI's Stance on Military Contracts

Dario Amodei, CEO of Anthropic, strongly criticized OpenAI's messaging regarding its Pentagon contract as "**outright lies**."

Anthropic abandoned its Pentagon contract due to AI safety concerns, but shortly afterward, OpenAI secured the deal. This dispute highlights the growing ideological divide within the industry over AI ethics, going beyond simple corporate competition to reflect fundamental differences in how AI technology should be developed and deployed.

**Impact on our lives:** Differences in corporate policies regarding military AI applications could influence future AI technology usage and regulation.

## Open-Source Genome Analysis AI Model Launched

A major advancement has occurred in genetic research. A large genome model trained on trillions of DNA base pairs has been released as open source.

This AI model can automate genetic analysis tasks that previously relied on manual work by human experts, including gene identification, control sequence recognition, and splice site detection. This represents a significant step toward accelerating life sciences research and realizing personalized medicine.

[→ Read the original article (Ars Technica)](https://arstechnica.com/science/2026/03/large-genome-model-open-source-ai-trained-on-trillions-of-bases/)

**Impact on our lives:** The speed and accuracy of genetic testing and personalized medicine will improve, potentially expanding disease prevention and treatment options.

## Trump Administration and Tech Companies Negotiate on Data Center Power Costs

On March 4, President Trump summoned leaders of major tech companies to the White House and had them sign a pledge to contain rising electricity costs around data centers.

Seven major tech firms—Google, Meta, Microsoft, Oracle, OpenAI, Amazon, and xAI—participated, demonstrating a willingness for public-private cooperation in maintaining the power infrastructure necessary to support the massive computational resources essential for AI development.

This represents a political response reflecting the reality that AI, particularly generative AI, depends on the construction and maintenance of large-scale data centers.

[→ Read the original article (The Verge)](https://www.theverge.com/news/889578/data-center-power-pledge-white-house-google-meta-microsoft)

**Impact on our lives:** Ensuring business continuity for AI companies allows for stable provision of AI services and continued development of new technologies.
