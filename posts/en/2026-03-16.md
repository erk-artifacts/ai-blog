## ByteDance Temporarily Halts Global Rollout of Video Generation AI "Seedance 2.0"

ByteDance has reportedly paused the global expansion of its high-performance video generation AI "Seedance 2.0" due to **copyright concerns**.

Seddance 2.0 was regarded as a "performance monster" due to its exceptional generative capabilities, but copyright infringement allegations regarding the training data for the generative AI have forced cautious handling of the situation.

This illustrates the deepening of a challenge facing the entire generative AI industry—copyright issues surrounding training data.

**Impact on our lives**: While video generation AI service rollouts may be delayed, this could also lead to strengthened copyright protections.

## Google and Accel Select Five "Non-Wrapper" Companies from 4,000+ AI Startups

Google and Accel jointly selected five final companies through the "Atoms" accelerator program, receiving over 4,000 India-related AI startup applications. Notably, **approximately 70% of applications were AI wrappers** (companies merely utilizing existing LLMs or APIs).

The program explicitly stated its policy of "excluding AI wrappers," selecting only startups capable of substantive technological innovation and providing genuinely new value.

This suggests strengthening market demand for "authentic AI innovation."

The AI industry in mid-March 2026 is entering a period where **advancement to practical implementation stages** and **addressing societal implementation challenges** proceed simultaneously.
