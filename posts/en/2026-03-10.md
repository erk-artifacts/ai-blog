## Anthropic Sues Trump Administration as AI Safety Concerns Spread

AI company Anthropic has filed a lawsuit against the Trump administration, claiming it faces retaliation for refusing to allow military and surveillance applications of its AI systems.

In response to the lawsuit, 37 employees from Google and OpenAI have submitted an "amicus brief" to support Anthropic. The filing is signed by industry leaders including Jeff Dean (Google's Chief Scientist and Gemini development lead), signaling strong concerns about the government's approach to AI regulation.

Reports indicate that AI was also used in attacks on Iran, heightening tensions surrounding military applications of generative AI technology.

**Impact on Our Lives:** The balance between free AI development and government regulation will significantly influence the pace and direction of future technological innovation.

## Sony Honda Mobility Establishes Independent Path with End-to-End Autonomous Driving AI Technology

Sony Honda Mobility has announced its policy to independently develop **End-to-End (E2E) AI**, the core technology for autonomous driving. This means avoiding the sharing of data and AI models with Honda and taking an independent approach.

The company is developing its own AI model that uses millimeter-wave radar and LiDAR (laser radar) in addition to cameras for training. With the electric vehicle "AFEELA," which boasts computing power exceeding Tesla's in the mass-market price range, it aims to demonstrate a new direction for software-defined vehicles (SDV).

**Impact on Our Lives:** As more advanced autonomous driving AI becomes available in mass-produced vehicles, safe and comfortable mobility experiences are becoming a reality.

## Virtual Staff Supporting 13 Languages: Generative AI Utilized in Hotel Check-In System

Device Agency Inc. has added a "Virtual Hospitality Navigation" feature to its self-check-in system for accommodations, "AdvaNceD IoT Smart Check-in."

## Summary

As of March 2026, the AI industry stands at a major turning point. On one hand, conflicts like the Anthropic versus Trump administration dispute highlight questions about regulating versus permitting military and surveillance uses of generative AI. On the other hand, major companies like Samsung and Sony Honda Mobility are integrating AI technology into products, accelerating AI's penetration into daily life.

With practical AI applications advancing, such as hotel check-in systems, we can say we have entered a stage where **generative AI is beginning to be used across all aspects of society**. The balance between government regulation and industrial promotion will hold the key to future AI development.
