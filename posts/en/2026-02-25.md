## National Diet Library Releases GPU-Free OCR Tool "NDLOCR-Lite"

The National Diet Library released an OCR tool (Optical Character Recognition tool) called "NDLOCR-Lite" on February 24 that runs on notebook computers. This tool is characterized by not requiring a GPU (Graphics Processing Unit) and can be easily used in a general PC environment. OCR is an AI technology that automatically recognizes text in scanned documents and images, with the potential to accelerate the digitization of many documents that have not yet been digitized.

*Impact on our lives*: Digitization of old documents and handwritten papers becomes easier, improving data management and search efficiency.

## AI Agent System for Network Maintenance Operations Begins Commercialization

Commercialization has begun for an **AI agent system for network maintenance operations** (an AI system that makes automatic decisions and executions) based on world-class scale data. This AI system automates and streamlines the maintenance of large-scale network infrastructure, resulting in reduced human resources and lower operational costs. By combining machine learning (ML) and data analysis, it enables network failure prediction and automatic repair.

## Successful Demonstration of Service AI Application Operations on vRAN Infrastructure

A demonstration of service AI application operations leveraging **virtualized RAN (vRAN)** infrastructure was successful. vRAN is a technology that softwarizes conventional physical communication base stations, enabling the construction of flexible and efficient 5G/6G communication infrastructure. The success of this AI operation demonstrates the potential for AI integration in next-generation communication networks.

[→ Read the original article (ASCII.jp)](https://ascii.jp/elem/000/004/376/4376461/?rss)

*Impact on our lives*: 5G/6G communications will become smarter and more efficient, enabling faster and more stable communication services.

## Agriculture × Generative AI Moves Toward Social Implementation: Solving the "Last Mile"

The adoption of **generative AI** in agriculture is advancing, with progress being made in solving the "last mile" (final challenge) that has been blocking social implementation. Generative AI is being utilized to support decision-making in agriculture, including crop disease and pest diagnosis, harvest timing prediction, and optimal fertilizer management. Overcoming challenges such as data shortage and implementation difficulties, practical applications in actual agricultural fields are expanding.

## Walking Rehabilitation Robotic Exoskeleton "inoGear HE-1" Pilot Facility Recruitment

A **robotic exoskeleton** for walking rehabilitation called "inoGear HE-1" has been developed, and recruitment of pilot facilities has begun. This robotic exoskeleton uses AI and machine learning to recognize the user's movements and optimize rehabilitation effects. It is medical robotics designed to support recovery in patients with walking difficulties due to stroke or spinal cord injury.

[→ Read the original article (ASCII.jp)](https://ascii.jp/elem/000/004/376/4376450/?rss)

*Impact on our lives*: Improved rehabilitation effectiveness increases opportunities for more people to regain walking function.
