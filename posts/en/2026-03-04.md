## Microsoft Build 2026 to Be Held in San Francisco, the Holy Land of AI

Microsoft announced that its annual developer conference "Build 2026" will be held in San Francisco starting June 2nd. The event is moving from its traditional home in Seattle to "the holy land of AI," with CEO Satya Nadella and other executives scheduled to speak. The venue will be Fort Mason, a former U.S. military base, and the keynote address will be streamed live starting at midnight Japan Standard Time on June 3rd.

Faced with the remarkable evolution of AI, balancing skill development and AI utilization has become a critical issue that will determine the competitiveness of entire organizations.

## Aikido to Conduct Demonstration of AI Data Center Placement at Offshore Wind Farm

Renewable energy utilization is accelerating in the AI industry. **Aikido**, an offshore wind power development company, plans to begin a demonstration project in 2026 to place an AI data center beneath floating offshore wind turbines.

This approach is attracting attention as an innovative method to directly power data centers' enormous electricity demands with clean energy. Unlike traditional land-based or space-based data centers, this approach leverages cooling efficiency at sea while integrating with renewable energy, enabling efficient power supply for training **AI learning models** (large language models and neural networks) required for AI development.

[→ Read the original article (TechCrunch AI)](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/04/who-needs-data-centers-in-space-when-they-can-float-offshore/)

*Impact on Our Lives*: The environmental impact of AI services may be reduced, and more sustainable AI development may become possible.
