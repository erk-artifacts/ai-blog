## Anthropic Faces Pressure from US Government as AI Safety Policy Dispute Escalates

The Trump administration has taken unprecedented harsh measures against Anthropic for refusing to abandon AI safety policies. Secretary of Defense Hegzes has instructed that the company be designated as a "supply chain risk," marking the first such designation applied to a US company. Anthropic shows no signs of changing its policy to maintain safety measures to prevent conversion for autonomous weapons, and is prepared to take the matter to court.

**What's the Issue?** Anthropic's implementation of technical restrictions to prevent unlimited military use of its foundational AI models, such as Claude (large language models = LLMs), has triggered government dissatisfaction.

**The Underlying Context** This dispute reflects a fundamental debate over balancing the social responsibility of AI development companies with national security. Anthropic maintains its stance on "preventing misuse of its own technology" and is in direct confrontation with the government's hardline approach.

## The "Trap" Anthropic Faces: Between Self-Regulation and Government Pressure
