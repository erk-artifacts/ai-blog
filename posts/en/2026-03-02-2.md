## What is Hallucination?

AI may provide incorrect information as if it were true.

Anthropic has compiled practical techniques to minimize <span style="font-size:125%;">「hallucinations」</span>, a phenomenon where AI outputs plausible falsehoods that contradict facts, which are summarized [here](https://platform.claude.com/docs/ja/test-and-evaluate/strengthen-guardrails/reduce-hallucinations). ## Strategies to Reduce AI Hallucinations

To increase the reliability of responses generated by AI and prevent hallucinations, careful crafting of prompts (instructions) is essential. We will explain this in two parts: "basic strategies" and "advanced techniques."

### 1. Basic Strategies (Prompt Engineering)

When having AI process information, explicitly instructing the following rules can greatly prevent the generation of uncertain information.

* **Grant permission to say "I don't know"**
    * **Explanation:** AI typically tends to force an answer and lie rather than admit uncertainty. Simply instructing in your prompt that "if there is insufficient information or you are not confident, answer with 'I do not have enough information'" can prevent pretending to know.
* **Use direct quotations to substantiate facts**
    * **Explanation:** When having AI read long documents, rather than immediately asking for an answer, instruct it to "first extract the sentences that form the basis of your answer word-for-word." By having the response created based solely on these extracted quotations, the content is backed by the actual text, and hallucinations decrease.
* **Use citations (sources) for verification**
    * **Explanation:** After AI generates a claim or response, instruct it to "find sentences from the original document that support that claim, cite them as sources, and if you cannot find them, delete that claim." This allows AI to fact-check its own answers.

### 2. Advanced Techniques

When higher precision answers or verification is required, the following approaches are effective.

* **Verification through chain-of-thought**
    * Rather than jumping directly to a final conclusion, instruct it to "explain your reasoning process step-by-step before providing an answer." This prevents logical leaps and flawed assumptions.
* **Comparison through multiple executions (Best-of-N verification)**
    * Execute the same prompt multiple times and compare the AI's outputs. If the content varies with each execution (lacks consistency), it is a strong indicator that hallucination is occurring.
* **Iterative refinement**
    * Use the AI's output as input for the next prompt and instruct it to "verify your previous response," allowing AI to identify and correct contradictions or errors on its own.
* **External knowledge restriction**
    * Strictly limit it by saying "do not use your general knowledge; use only information from the provided documents to answer."

---

**💡 Important Note**

While these techniques can significantly reduce the risk of hallucinations, it is important to note that "it is impossible to eliminate them entirely." Especially for information involved in critical decision-making, it is recommended that humans themselves always perform final validation to confirm accuracy.
