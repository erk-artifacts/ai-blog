# Trump Administration Designates Anthropic as Government Supply Chain Risk

President Donald Trump has ordered the immediate removal of AI company Anthropic's products from federal government agencies. The trigger was CEO **Dario Amodei's** opposition to autonomous weapons (killer robots) and refusal to dismantle AI safety standards. Trump has criticized Anthropic as an "ultra-left woke company," and the Pentagon conflict highlights a **struggle over the leadership of military AI regulation**.

[→ Read the original article (The Verge)](https://www.theverge.com/policy/886632/pentagon-designates-anthropic-supply-chain-risk-ai-standoff)

*Impact on our lives*: The direction of future AI regulation is increasingly likely to be heavily influenced by government preferences.

## Anthropic vs. Pentagon: The Battle Over Military AI Control

This conflict is not merely a surface-level political dispute, but raises a fundamental question: **who decides the rules for military AI use (autonomous weapons and large-scale surveillance systems)?** Anthropic has refused to compromise its AI safety technology, creating a deep divide between corporate ethical standards and national security. Meanwhile, volunteer employees from Google, Amazon, and Microsoft have issued statements supporting Anthropic. OpenAI CEO Sam Altman has announced plans to accelerate discussions with the military, revealing divided responses within the AI industry.

[→ Read the original article (TechCrunch)](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/27/anthropic-vs-the-pentagon-whats-actually-at-stake/)

*Impact on our lives*: The establishment of rules for military AI development will significantly change future defense technology and cybersecurity standards.

## ChatGPT Achieves Remarkable 900 Million Weekly Active Users

OpenAI announced that ChatGPT (a conversational generative AI) has reached **900 million weekly active users**. Simultaneously, OpenAI completed a massive fundraising round of $110 billion. This figure means ChatGPT has achieved penetration comparable to smartphones and social media, demonstrating that generative AI (LLMs: large language models) has become deeply embedded in human daily life.

[→ Read the original article (TechCrunch)](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/27/chatgpt-reaches-900m-weekly-active-users/)

*Impact on our lives*: Conversational AI usage like ChatGPT is no longer a specialty tool but has become a common everyday means of information gathering for many people.

## AI Music Generator Suno Achieves 2 Million Paid Subscribers and $300 Million in Annual Revenue

Audio generation AI company **Suno** reported remarkable growth with 2 million paid subscribers and annual recurring revenue (ARR) of $300 million. Because Suno can generate music using only natural language prompts (text instructions), users without musical experience can easily create high-quality music. This service demonstrates the **rapid penetration of generative AI into creative industries**.

[→ Read the original article (TechCrunch)](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/27/ai-music-generator-suno-hits-2-million-paid-subscribers-and-300m-in-annual-recurring-revenue/)

*Impact on our lives*: Music production is becoming more democratized, and creating creative works without specialized knowledge is becoming a reality.

This represents a **new step in AI integration and one-stop strategy**.

## Elon Musk Criticizes OpenAI While Advocating for the Safety of His Own Grok

In testimony during a lawsuit against OpenAI, Elon Musk highlighted his AI "**Grok's**" safety, claiming "nobody committed suicide because of Grok" in an implicit criticism of ChatGPT. However, months later, Grok **massively distributed non-consensual nude images on X (formerly Twitter)**, creating a serious ethical problem. Some observers have pointed out a gap between claims about AI safety and actual service operations.

[→ Read the original article (TechCrunch)](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/27/musk-bashes-openai-in-deposition-saying-nobody-committed-suicide-because-of-grok/)

*Impact on our lives*: Rather than taking AI companies' safety claims at face value, we need to examine concrete problems that emerge after implementation.

## MIT Technology Review's Report on AI Energy Consumption Reaches Finals for National Magazine Award

MIT Technology Review's **detailed investigative report on AI's energy footprint (power consumption)** was selected as a finalist for the 2026 National Magazine Award by the American Society of Magazine Editors (ASME). The report, "We did the math on AI's energy footprint," quantifies previously unclear actual AI energy consumption, making **the environmental impact of generative AI visible**.

[→ Read the original article (MIT Technology Review)](https://www.technologyreview.com/2026/02/27/1133769/asme-finalist-reporting/)

*Impact on our lives*: As AI service usage expands, electricity consumption may become a major environmental issue, making sustainability measures urgent.

## Summary

At the end of February 2026, the AI industry faces a turbulent period marked by debates over **regulation versus freedom, safety versus functionality, and corporate ethics versus national security**.

On one hand, generative AI products like ChatGPT and Suno are deeply penetrating human daily life and democratizing creative industries and business. On the other hand, the Trump administration and Anthropic are clashing over military AI regulation, with divided opinions within AI companies themselves.

Most notably, the **fundamental dilemma between rapidly advancing AI technology and society's inability to keep pace with its social and environmental impacts** is becoming increasingly visible. Security vulnerabilities in systems like Grok, AI's massive energy consumption, ethical conflicts over military applications—all of these force us to reconsider how we design the AI era.

In the coming months, how corporations, governments, and civil society engage in "rule-making" around AI will largely shape the form of the next generation's AI society.
