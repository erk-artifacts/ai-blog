## AWS Releases "Kiro 0.9" with Sub-agent Feature Enhancement

Amazon Web Services announced the release of the new version "Kiro 0.9" of its AI code editor "Kiro". This version adds **functionality that allows you to assign frontend and backend development responsibilities to each sub-agent (AI agents handling partial tasks)**. This enables more efficient automation of complex software development projects.

[→ Read the original article (ITmedia AI+)](https://www.itmedia.co.jp/news/articles/2602/27/news086.html)

*Impact on our lives*: Software development productivity will improve, and more complex applications may reach the market faster.

## Abandoned School in Kagawa Prefecture Transforms into AI Data Center—Facility Equipped with NVIDIA's Latest GPUs Opens in March

Hyreso, a GPU cloud service company, announced that it will **open an AI data center on March 3rd**, converting an abandoned school in Ayauta County, Kagawa Prefecture. The data center is scheduled to be equipped with NVIDIA's latest AI GPUs "B200" and "H200". By utilizing existing buildings to reduce construction costs, this strategic approach aims to **realize affordable cloud GPU services**.

[→ Read the original article (ITmedia AI+)](https://www.itmedia.co.jp/news/articles/2602/27/news084.html)

*Impact on our lives*: Idle facilities in regional areas are being transformed into AI infrastructure, contributing to regional economic revitalization and enrichment of Japan's overall AI computing infrastructure.

## Backlog AI Assistant Officially Launches March 5th—Realizes Efficiency in Task Management Operations

The project management tool "Backlog" will **officially release its AI assistant feature "Backlog AI Assistant" on March 5th**. According to early adopters, this AI feature can significantly streamline complex management tasks such as **automatic identification of high-risk delay tasks** and **monitoring team member status**. This drastically reduces the confirmation work that was previously done manually.

*Impact on our lives*: Team members' project management burdens will decrease, allowing them to spend more time on creative work.

## LogEater Implementation Reduces Jigen Inc.'s Log Investigation Work to 1/20th

Jigen Inc. has **implemented "LogEater," a log analysis AI agent**. Server log investigation work that previously took several hours is now **completed in just minutes** with the AI agent, according to the company. Additionally, previously undetected threats that human eyes might have missed can now be discovered.
